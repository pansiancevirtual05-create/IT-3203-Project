HTML Introduction

One of the most important computer languages not talked about as much, but widely used and often overshadowed by programming languages is HTML [HyperText Markup Language]. This language makes it possible for websites and browsers to be accessible, organized, and interconnected on the World Wide Web. In this essay, I am writing about HTML. While people often focus on the programming of modern apps built with Python, Java, etc., they often don't bat an eye at the web browser they use or look beyond it. HTML is a structure for all websites and web browsers, it is the foundation on which the World Wide Web is built. It’s been used since 1991 and is used by most websites and web browsers ever made. It is very easy to use alongside other programming languages like CSS and JavaScript.

To understand it better, I like to think of a website like building a car, so HTML would be the metal frame, it gives the car its shape. CSS is the colors displayed on the car, the aesthetic of the seats, and the shiny rims that give the car its style. JavaScript is the engine and the electronics; it is what allows the car to accelerate, brake, and turn on the air conditioning. Without HTML, you don't have the structure of the car to begin with; you just have an engine and paint. The three work together, but HTML is the OG that started it all. The development of HTML has made website connections and experiences very rich. The purpose of this essay is to explain the role and purpose of HTML, discuss its history from the early days at CERN to the current browser wars, and look at the future directions of this great piece of technology.
